### How to Calculate Labor Costs

Knowing how much you spend on labor helps you make better business decisions. Here’s how to figure out your labor costs:

**1. Count All Wages:** Add up all the money you pay your staff, including part-time and full-time people. This includes all - full-time and part-time workers too. If you are paying them by the hour then you will need to calculate the wages by hours worked.

**2. Include Benefits:** Things like health insurance and paid time off also cost money. Add these to your total.

**3. Add Taxes and Other Costs:** Don’t forget about payroll taxes and other costs like workers’ compensation insurance. Also include payroll taxes per employee. This will help you calculate the actual cost of an employee that your cleaning company is handling.

**4. Factor in Overtime:** If your team works extra hours, include this in your costs too. There are different companies that pay their employees in a different manner if they work overtime.

Once you get the number you calculated for a period, it is the number you will be paying to the employee. By knowing these numbers, you can see if you need to make changes to save money. This is a direct and simple way to calculate the cost. But sometimes indirect costs occur as well.

## Tips to Reduce Labor Costs in the Cleaning Industry

Using software specifically for cleaning businesses can really help control labor costs. Here’s how it works:

* **[Automated Scheduling](https://novagems.com/scheduling-management-for-everyone/):** This tool helps plan out everyone’s day so you can avoid paying for overtime and ensure jobs are lined up back-to-back efficiently.
* **Task Management:** The software tells each team member what to do and tracks how long tasks take. This helps everyone stay on task without wasting time.
* **Keep Everyone Connected:** With features like messaging and job updates, your team can stay in touch throughout the day. This cuts down on lost time and confusion.
* **Offer Good Benefits to Reduce Employee Turnover:** If your employees are happy with your company, they are more likely to stay. This way they will not switch constantly and you will not have to spend money in hiring and training new employees.
* **Prevent Wage Creeping:** Wage creeping occurs when pay rates increase without an equivalent increase in productivity, often due to small, incremental raises given broadly rather than strategically. To prevent wage creeping, conduct regular reviews of wage structures against market standards and productivity rates. Ensure that increases in pay are aligned with clear, measurable enhancements in performance or responsibilities.
* **Encourage Productivity with Incentives:** One of the most effective ways to motivate your employees is through incentives. Offering good rewards and bonuses encourages the team to perform well. You can give rewards on some jobs well done or completing a certain number of jobs in a week. When you see an increase in productivity, labor costs will automatically reduce.
* **Train Your Team:** Well-trained employees work faster and make fewer mistakes, which saves money. Improved productivity means lower labor costs. Well-trained employees are skilled enough to handle multiple things at once. You will not need to hire multiple people and you can save some bucks this way too.
* **[Use Checklists](https://novagems.com/instructions-checklist/):** Having a list of tasks for each job helps workers stay focused and ensures nothing is forgotten. This is very helpful in commercial cleaning as the cleaners have a lot of tasks to deal with. They can simply tick off the tasks as they complete them. This will make them feel a deep sense of satisfaction once everything is done.
* **Plan Travel Smartly:** Try to schedule jobs that are near each other on the same day to save on travel time and costs. Because you are the owner of the cleaning services you might have to pay the staff their travel expenses.
* **Reduce Overtime Pay:** Efficiently scheduling staff is important for controlling labor costs. Scheduling too many employees can lead to idle time and overstaffing. While too few employees can result in the need for overtime to complete jobs on time. The overworked employee can ask for overtime pay or extra cash and you will be legally bound to pay. And increasing labor costs.
