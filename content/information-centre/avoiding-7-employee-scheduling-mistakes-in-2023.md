### Mistake #1: Not Planning Ahead

This is one of the biggest mistakes businesses make when it comes to scheduling. Don't wait until the last minute to start planning your schedules. The earlier you start, the more options you'll have and the less likely you are to make mistakes.

If you do not have a clear plan ahead you will be left scrambling for replacements at the end moment. An employee scheduling software will allow you to make schedules in advance, make any adjustments according to the needs and send instant notifications to the employees.

### Mistake #2: Not Considering Employee Availability

If you want to boost the overall productivity of the business, you must have an effective way to track the availability of the employees. Make sure to take into account your employees' personal commitments, such as school, childcare, and other obligations. Don't schedule them for shifts that they can't work.

This will result in high employee turnover rate, reduced productivity and less [employee satisfaction](https://novagems.com/information-centre/remote-work-and-employee-engagement-best-practices-for-a-connected-team/). To avoid the mistake of not considering employee availability, businesses should implement effective scheduling practices. 

Manage employee schedules easily with a scheduling software that will allow you to check when a particular employee is unavailable for the shift. If the software allows, you can even give your employees the power to select the desired shift. 

### Mistake #3: Not Communicating Scheduling Changes Effectively

Once you've created your schedules, be sure to communicate them to your employees in a timely and clear manner. This will help to avoid confusion and also prevent employee scheduling conflicts. 

Even if you have a general idea of the availability of the employees, if you do not communicate properly the chances of error increase. Once a schedule is made, an employee scheduling software must send the notifications so that the employees are aware of their schedules and they can plan ahead. To avoid this mistake, businesses should prioritize clear and timely communication of scheduling changes.

If the employees are made aware of their schedule at the last minute this could lead to employee frustration, operational disruptions and last minute no shows.

### Mistake #6: Not Allowing For Flexibility

Things don't always go according to plan, so it's important to be flexible with your schedules. Be prepared to make changes as needed, such as when employees call in sick or have to take time off for personal reasons.

Prevent employee scheduling conflicts easily if you allow flexibility to a certain extent. Do not allow the employees to work overtime and do not allow them to work less than required hours. With optimum flexibility, the employees can enjoy a balanced work schedule, have high morale, and overall enjoy working. 

This allows the organizations to retain their high performing employees. Having an open communication plays a crucial role in flexibility as the managers need to check that there is no time theft happening.
