## What is Workplace Violence?

Workplace violence is any act or threat of physical harm, harassment, intimidation, or disruptive behavior occurring at the worksite. It includes:

* Physical attacks
* Threats, spoken or written
* Aggressive gestures
* Harassment or intimidation
* Property Damage
* Domestic disputes spill into the workplace

Workplace violence is considered among the top causes of fatal injury in various industries by major safety agencies. The reality is this: There were 5,283 fatal work injuries recorded in the United States in 2023, a 3.7-percent decrease from 5,486 in 2022, the [U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cfoi.nr0.htm) reported.

Most incidents do not begin with sudden aggression. They escalate gradually, showing measurable warning signs en route. For this reason, among many, workplace violence prevention is no longer an option but an obligation to provide a safe environment that protects employees, visitors, and assets. Security guards are often the first layer of defense. Their capacity to identify early red flags directly impacts how quickly violence can be prevented.

## What are the early warning signs of workplace violence?

Knowing the early warning signs helps guards to intervene before an incident escalates. Employers often ask the question, "how guards can identify warning signs early?" because they need to be prepared. These signs typically fall into one of three categories: behavioral, verbal, and situational.

### 1. Behavioral Warning Signs

These often appear subtly, yet they signal rising frustration or emotional instability.

* Unusual anger, agitation, or irritability
* Withdrawing from coworkers or social interactions
* Excessive sweating, pacing, or shaking
* Abrupt changes in attendance or performance
* Preoccupation with past hurts
* Paranoia: ("Everyone is against me")
* Staring aggressively at people
* Overreacting to minor issues

A change in behavior may reflect increased tension or a possible aggressive attitude.

### 2. Verbal Warning Signs

People often express frustration prior to acting on it:

* Verbal threats against colleagues, customers, or management
* Talking about past violent incidents
* Complaints of personal injustice: "I'll make them regret it."
* Jokes about harming others
* Hopelessness - "I have nothing left to lose"
* Warning others to stay away

Guards should consider verbal threats seriously, even if the person “appears harmless.”

### 3. Situational & Environmental Warning Signs

* Risk factors that relate to the job role or workplace environment include
* High-stress work conditions
* Recent lay-offs or demotions
* History of conflict between employees
* Domestic abuse situations involving an employee
* Access to unsecured entry points
* Poor lighting or lack of supervision

When part of a constellation of behavioral or verbal warning signs, situational factors significantly elevate the risk of violence.

## How Can Security Guards Prevent Workplace Violence: 10 Tips That Work

Because security guards have unique visibility into daily activity, their role is highly relevant in how to prevent workplace violence. The following are some effective strategies guards can use immediately:

### 1. Remain observant and scan for warning signs

Use the early warning indicators listed above to identify suspicious behavior. Guards should notice:

* Tone of voice
* Body Language
* Changes to routine
* Tense interactions among employees
* The best prevention is usually simple awareness.

### 2. Build Rapport With Employees

Guards who greet employees daily are more likely to notice changes in

* Mood
* Appearance
* Behavioral patterns

This creates opportunities for early intervention and building trust.

### 3. Address Conflicts Early

If guards observe disagreements or increased arguing between prisoners:

* Approach calmly
* Separate individuals
* Convey the need for safety
* Report the problem immediately

Small conflicts, if neglected, can spiral very fast.

### 5. Monitoring High-Risk Zones

Certain areas that require more attention include:

* Entry and exit points
* Regular patrols deter would-be criminals.

### 6. Implement Access Control

Violent confrontations can arise from unauthorized access. Guards should:

* Check IDs
* Monitoring visitor logs
* Lock restricted areas

### 8. Remain calm and use de-escalation techniques

When confronted with aggressive behavior, guards should:

* Keep the tone neutral.
* Keep a safe distance

* Listen actively
* Offer alternatives

De-escalation averts numerous violent results.

Guards shall report:

* Behavioural changes

When a situation becomes unsafe, the guards should:

### The Employer's Role in Addressing Workplace Violence

Workplace violence prevention is a joint responsibility. Employers must set an environment that allows guards to work effectively.

**1. Create Clear Policies on Workplace Violence**

Policies should explain:

* Zero-tolerance policies
* Reporting expectations
* Threat Escalation Procedures
* Disciplinary actions

Every employee should be aware of the guidelines.

**2. Training and Refresher Courses**

* Regular training enables the staff to identify:
* Warning signs
* How to report threats
* De-escalation Techniques
* Emergency Response Actions

Well-trained teams respond best under pressure.

**3. Promote a Speak-up Culture**

Often, employees know that there are problems before the guards do. Employers should:

* Offer anonymous reporting avenues
* Respond promptly to concerns
* Reward Proactive Behaviour

It ignores possible threats, making prevention impossible.

**4. Support Mental Health**

Providing access to:

* Counseling
* stress management programs
* Workplace Wellness Resources
* It can reduce incidents significantly.

**5. Equip Security Teams with Proper Tools**

This includes:

* Surveillance systems
* Radios
* Access control panels
* Security guard management software for violence prevention

Technology bridges this gap between detection and response.
