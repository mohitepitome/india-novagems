**Foot Patrol** 

These kinds of patrols are usually done by the security guard on foot. This gives them a better understanding of the area and they can familiarize themselves with the area. One of the primary roles of foot patrol security guards is to act as a visible deterrent to potential criminals. The mere presence of a security guard can discourage criminal activity, reducing the risk of theft, vandalism, and trespassing.

In the event of an emergency, such as a fire, medical incident, or security breach, foot patrol security guards are often the first responders. Their training equips them to assess situations quickly and take appropriate action to protect lives and property. Mobile patrol services usually come handy when foot patrol security guards are patrolling the area.

Foot patrol security guards can quickly seek assistance from their superiors if they encounter any criminal activity happening. While technological advancements like surveillance cameras and access control systems have undoubtedly improved security measures, they cannot completely replace the human touch. Foot patrol security guards bring a unique blend of skills and qualities to the table. Foot patrol security may not be able to cover a wider area, but they can adapt to changing circumstances and unexpected challenges

Mobile patrols are conducted with the help of equipment such as automobiles, bicycles, ATVs, and golf carts. Through mobile patrols, the guards can cover far wider areas.
