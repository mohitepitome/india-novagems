## Understand the Types of Security Contracts You Can Bid For

Before diving into applications, it’s essential to understand the main types of security contracts available. Knowing your ideal client type will help you choose the right strategy and submit stronger proposals.

* ### Commercial Security Contracts

These are contracts with private businesses and organizations, such as:

* Office complexes
* Retail malls
* Manufacturing units
* Logistics warehouses

These contracts are usually long-term, recurring, and often have standard operating hours, making them a stable source of income.

* ### Government & Municipal Contracts

This is where you get government security contracts. These opportunities often include:

* Courthouses
* City halls
* Municipal utilities
* Public parks and events

They require more paperwork, licenses, and certifications, but offer higher value and longer durations.

* ### Event & Venue-Based Security Services

Short-term, high-intensity contracts for:

* Concerts
* Sports events
* Conferences
* Political rallies

They’re great for boosting your portfolio and collecting testimonials quickly, even if they don’t offer long-term cash flow.

## Where to Find Security Contract Opportunities

Knowing where to find security contracts is half the battle. Here are the most effective and active platforms in 2025:

1. ### SAM.gov – For Federal Contracts

If you're serious about scaling, [SAM.gov](http://sam.gov) is the official place to get government security contracts. You can register your company, browse security-related opportunities, and filter by agency or location.

[ https://sam.gov](https://sam.gov)

### BidNet Direct – State & Local Bids

BidNet aggregates bids from cities, counties, and state departments. You can sign up for alerts by category (like "security guard services") to stay ahead.

<https://www.bidnetdirect.com>

2. ### InstantMarkets.com – Keyword-Specific Alerts

Set custom alerts for "security patrol", "guard services", or "mobile patrol contracts". It saves time and keeps you updated.

[ https://www.instantmarkets.com](https://www.instantmarkets.com)

3. ### Construction Bid Boards – for Construction Site Security

Construction firms often outsource site security. Browse boards like:

* Dodge Construction Central
* The Blue Book
* iSqFt

You’ll find ongoing security needs for large construction projects.

4. ### Local Government Portals – Counties & Municipalities

Check your city and county government websites for local tenders. These are usually less competitive and ideal if you're starting small.

## How to Write Winning Proposals That Clients Remember

A smart bid isn’t just about price, it’s about trust, professionalism, and clarity. These security company proposal tips will help you get noticed:

### Highlight Experience & Licenses

* Years of operation
* Staff qualifications
* Local or federal licenses
* Insurance coverage

Even if you’re a new company, emphasize management's background or specialized training (e.g., CPR-certified, armed guard licenses).

### Emphasize Technology (Guard Tour, Reporting, Real-Time Alerts)

Clients expect transparency. Mention:

* GPS guard tracking
* Guard tour systems
* Incident alerts and daily activity reports

This can be a game-changer for your proposal, especially if you use solutions like Novagems.

### Break Down Pricing Transparently

Don’t give a lump sum. Instead, break down:

* Hourly rates per shift
* Weekend or holiday surcharges
* Equipment/uniform fees
* Taxes or optional add-ons

Transparency reduces quote objections and shows professionalism.

### Include SLA, Insurance, & Accountability Clauses

Show clients you’re serious about performance and legal compliance:

* Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
* Insurance certificates
* Supervisor contact details
* Shift check-in processes

## Stand Out Without Being the Lowest Bid

Winning a contract isn’t about undercutting everyone else. It’s about showing more value.

### Offer Real-Time Guard Reporting (GPS, Check-ins, Daily Logs)

Instead of relying on manual reports, offer clients:

* Mobile check-in logs
* Patrol verification
* Automated shift summaries

It builds trust and helps clients stay compliant too.

### Show Historical Success With Similar Clients (Case Studies)

Even a short win matters. Example:

"Our mobile patrol team secured a local shopping complex, reducing vandalism incidents by 60% over 4 months."

Real stats = real trust.

### Add Value: Site Risk Assessments, Post-Assignment Briefings

Bonus services add perceived value:

* Initial risk assessments
* Emergency preparedness reports
* End-of-month site review meetings

It shows you’re not just a guard vendor, you’re a partner in safety.

## Build Long-Term Relationships with Clients

Winning the contract is one thing, keeping it is another. Here’s how to turn short-term clients into repeat business:

### Focus on Responsiveness

Replying to emails or calls within 1 hour can differentiate you from 90% of security vendors. 

### Create Post-Contract Follow-Up Processes

Build check-in habits:

* 30-day satisfaction call
* Quarterly performance review
* Annual renewal planning

It shows you care beyond the contract start date.

### Request Testimonials + Google Reviews

After 60–90 days of service, ask happy clients for:

* A Google review
* A 2–3 sentence testimonial
* A short case study quote for your proposals

It strengthens your authority and trust with new prospects.
