### Guard Patrol App: How It Works 

Guard tour systems employ three main technologies for efficient monitoring:

**RFID Technology:** RFID tags are strategically placed at checkpoints within the facility. When a guard scans a checkpoint, the RFID tag emits a unique signal received by the device. This interaction records the scan's time and date, and the data is sent to the central server for analysis.

**QR Code Scanning:** QR codes, those square barcodes you often see, are positioned at various points in the facility. Guards use their handheld devices to scan these QR codes. Upon scanning, the device notes the time and date of the interaction. Which is then transmitted to the central server for supervisors to review.

**GPS Tracking:** [GPS technology](https://novagems.com/gps-tracking/) relies on satellites to pinpoint the location of the guard's handheld device. The device captures this location data, which is then uploaded to the central server. This method ensures accurate tracking of the guard's movements, enhancing overall security measures.

These technologies work seamlessly together. Providing an advanced and reliable way to monitor security patrols. This helps to ensure the safety of the premises and its occupants.

#### What are the different types of guard patrol apps? 

Guard patrol apps come in two primary types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions.

**Cloud-based apps:** These Guard Patrol APP operate on remote servers and can be accessed from anywhere as long as there is an internet connection. They offer flexibility and accessibility, making them ideal for businesses with multiple locations. Security teams can easily monitor patrols and access data in real-time. Which helps in enhancing overall efficiency.

**On-premises apps:** In contrast, on-premises guard patrol apps are installed directly on the business's own servers. This setup provides the business with greater control over the application and its data. While it may require more initial setup. Businesses often opt for on-premises solutions when they prioritize having complete authority over their security data and infrastructure.
