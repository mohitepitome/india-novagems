## 2. Understand Your Potential Leads

An average customer does not just open Google and decide to leverage your cleaning services. Instead, there are different stages and touchpoints where the customer interacts without your business in this process. 

Have a clearly defined map of the entire customer journey. You can use this map to determine gaps and take corrective measures to ensure your visitors turn into paying customers. For example, if a customer has only enquired about the types of services you offer or prices you charge and never called you back. You can call them back and discuss the offers further. Find out what stops them from hiring you and incorporate better offers or discounts to win the customer.

A maid service business does not run single-handedly. Instead, the collaboration of the entire team, including the cleaning staff and managers, makes everything possible. However, it is often the case that most responsibilities fall on the shoulders of the manager or the business owner.
