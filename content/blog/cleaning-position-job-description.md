## What is a Cleaning Job Description?

A cleaning job description is a written document that outlines the duties, responsibilities, skills, and expectations for a cleaner, janitor, or sanitation technician. It’s your first impression on potential hires and should be tailored to reflect both your company culture and the job's demands.

**Introduction**

The introduction to the cleaning position job description should provide a brief overview of the position, its importance within the company, and the main objectives of the role. This section sets the stage for potential applicants, giving them a snapshot of what to expect.

**Job Title and Summary**

Clearly stating the job title and providing a concise summary helps candidates quickly understand the role they are applying for. The summary should highlight the primary responsibilities and the overall purpose of the position. The job title and summary must be easy to read and understand. You might lose many good candidates if they do not understand your requirements. 

**Example:**

**Summary:** The Cleaner is responsible for maintaining the cleanliness and hygiene of our facilities. Duties include sweeping, mopping, dusting, and sanitizing surfaces to ensure a safe and pleasant environment for staff and visitors.

## Key Responsibilities of a Cleaning Position

This section should detail the main tasks and duties associated with the position. Being specific about daily, specialized, and equipment-related responsibilities will help candidates understand the scope of the job. This will save yours and the candidates valuable time and you will not have to screen millions of resumes. 

* **Daily Cleaning Tasks**

Outline the routine cleaning duties that the employee will perform on a daily basis. This is especially important if you are posting a residential cleaner job description. There your cleaners will need to perform some day-to-day activities. So it is better that the candidate is aware beforehand. 

**Example:**

1. Sweep, mop, and vacuum floors
2. Dust and wipe down surfaces
3. Empty trash cans and replace liners
4. Clean and sanitize restrooms
5. Restock restroom supplies

* **Specialized Cleaning Duties**

Include any tasks that require special attention or are performed less frequently. So what are the duties of a residential cleaner that need to be specially mentioned? 

**Example:**

1. Clean windows and mirrors
2. Shampoo carpets
3. Polish furniture and fixtures
4. Perform deep cleaning of kitchen and break areas
5. Handle emergency cleaning tasks as needed

* **Equipment and Supplies Management**

Detail the responsibilities related to handling and maintaining cleaning equipment and supplies.

**Example:**

1. Operate and maintain cleaning machinery such as floor buffers and carpet cleaners
2. Ensure proper storage and handling of cleaning supplies and chemicals
3. Monitor inventory levels and report shortages to the supervisor
4. Follow safety protocols when using cleaning agents and equipment

### Additional Information 

#### Residential vs. Commercial Cleaner Duties

Now you must have seen the repetition of residential and commercial cleaners in this blog post. There is a clear difference in what is expected of them and it should be clearly visible in the job description as well. Let me break it down for you.

* #### Residential Cleaner Duties

Residential cleaners focus on cleaning private homes. Their tasks are typically more detailed and personalized, aiming to meet the specific needs of homeowners. They have direct interactions with your clients. Keep in mind that the cleaners have perfect soft skills while hiring them. 

**Key Focus:**

* **Personalization:** Taking care of specific requests of homeowners.
* **Attention to Detail:** Ensuring high standards of cleanliness and tidiness in living spaces.
* #### Commercial Cleaner Duties

Commercial cleaners focus on larger spaces such as offices, retail stores, and industrial buildings. Their tasks are often more routine and less personalized. They aim to maintain a clean and safe environment for employees and customers. It is not usually the work of one or two cleaners. 

**Key Focus:**

* **Routine Maintenance:** They make sure that the public spaces and workspaces are clean and hygiene to use.
* **Efficiency:** Covering large areas quickly and effectively.

### Janitor Job Responsibilities: Commercial Settings

Janitors typically work in schools, office buildings, hospitals, or shopping centers. Here's what to include when hiring for these environments:

* Monitor public restrooms for cleanliness and supply levels
* Lock doors, turn off lights, and ensure secure premises at the end of shifts
* Handle minor repairs or report facility issues to maintenance
* Maintain floor safety by using wet floor signs and correct signage
* Follow evening or night cleaning schedules strictly

### How to Write a Cleaning Job Description for USA/Canada Market

When targeting talent in Canada or the U.S., consider these regional and legal expectations:

#### Mention Labor Guidelines

* In the U.S., refer to OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)
* In Canada, include WHMIS (Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System)

#### Align with Worker Priorities

* Include wage range in local currency
* Highlight benefits like insurance or paid leave
* Emphasize job stability and training programs
