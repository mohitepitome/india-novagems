## Why Guard Scheduling is More Challenging Than You Think

On the surface, putting together a schedule might look easy: distribute shifts, ensure every location is covered, and roll it out to the team. But when you're actually in the job, you realize that it's a whole lot more complex.

**Unexpected Absences:** Security is always an uncertain thing. A guard gets sick, has some family crisis, or gets held up in traffic, and before you know it, you're frantically filling the void.

**Overtime and Compliance:** In North America, maximum hours are strictly regulated by labor rules and overtime pay. Without proper checks, it is too easy to overbook guards at the expense of compliance breakdowns and bloated payroll.

**Varied Client Requirements:** One client might demand licensed guards, another could have a bilingual workforce requirement, and another requires 24/7 coverage. Manually juggling these requirements is draining.

**The Issue with Spreadsheets:** Spreadsheets or rosters on paper appear to be convenient, but they take hours each week and are error-prone. A single misplaced entry can result in unserved shifts or dual bookings.

**Communication Breakdowns:** Schedules that are sent via WhatsApp or phone calls tend to get lost. Guards will not be updated in time, resulting in confusion or no-shows.

**Effect on Guard Morale:** Last-minute repeated requests and unreliable schedules lead guards to feel underappreciated. This demoralizes and frequently causes increased turnover, generating still more scheduling issues.

That is, security guard scheduling is more than filling in boxes on a calendar. It's juggling people, compliance, and client expectations, all while under pressure to provide consistent coverage.

According to [recent research](https://www.articlecube.com/research-shows-searching-information-work-wastes-time-and-money), companies stand to lose as much as 20% of productive time because of improper scheduling. For security companies, that translates directly to profit and customer satisfaction.

No surprise, then, that increasingly companies in the USA and Canada are transitioning to security guard scheduling software.

8. **Scalable for Growth:** No matter how many guards or sites you have, 20 or 2,000, cloud-based systems scale with you. Hiring additional staff or sites does not increase complexity.
