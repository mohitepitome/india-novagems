## Pick a Profitable Niche to Win More Cleaning Contracts

If you're wondering how to win cleaning contracts, it starts with knowing exactly who you're trying to serve. One of the biggest mistakes small cleaning businesses make is trying to be everything to everyone. But when you specialize, you increase trust and become more attractive to high-paying clients.

### Great Niches for 2025:

* Medical Offices – Require high hygiene standards, consistent cleaning schedules, and are less price-sensitive.
* Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb, Vrbo) – Fast turnaround jobs with frequent demand and good profit margins.
* Schools & Daycares – Recurring revenue with long-term relationships and high reliability.
* Office Buildings – Ideal for commercial cleaning contracts with fixed weekly or monthly schedules.
* Fitness Centers – Constant cleaning needs due to hygiene and foot traffic.

When you focus on one niche, your marketing becomes sharper and more effective. For example, “We help Airbnb hosts get 5-star reviews with reliable turnover cleanings” speaks directly to a pain point.

Want to grow your cleaning business this year? Pick a niche, learn its pain points, and position yourself as the expert they’ve been looking for.

## Build a Targeted Prospecting List to Win Commercial Cleaning Contracts

Waiting around for referrals? That’s a slow game. The fastest path to getting more cleaning clients is proactive outreach. In 2025, outbound prospecting is still one of the best strategies to land commercial cleaning contracts.

Here’s how to do it right:

#### Step 1: Find Local Leads

* Search Google Maps and Yelp for businesses in your niche (e.g., "medical offices in Brooklyn").
* Collect names, emails, and phone numbers.
* Use [Apollo.io](http://apollo.io) or [Hunter.io](http://hunter.io) to find verified contact info for decision-makers.
* On LinkedIn, search for roles like "office manager," "facility coordinator," or "property manager" in your city.

#### Step 2: Organize the Data

* Store leads in a spreadsheet or CRM like HubSpot or Notion.
* Track every touchpoint (email sent, follow-up, meeting booked).

By consistently prospecting, you're building a pipeline of warm leads who can eventually convert. This is not just how you get one job, it’s how to grow your cleaning business by systematizing client acquisition.

## Perfect Your Pitch & Proposal to Get More Cleaning Clients

You’ve got the leads, now you need to close them. This is where most cleaning companies fall flat. If you want to know how to win cleaning contracts, focus on improving your pitch and proposal game.

#### Your Pitch Should Be:

* Short and clear (30 seconds or less)
* Problem-solving (what benefit do you offer?)
* Specific to their industry

Example:

“We help property managers reduce cleaning complaints by 70% while maintaining 5-star tenant reviews using our 24/7 support and checklists.”

#### A Strong Proposal Should Include:

* Who you are and why they should trust you
* Scope of services (daily, weekly, deep cleans)
* Supplies and safety protocols used
* Price breakdowns and optional tiers
* Terms of service, including notice periods and inspection policies

Use clean formatting, your company logo, and the client’s name. Make it easy to scan, with bold headlines and bullet points.

Pro tip: Offer a PDF proposal template download on your site to build credibility and also capture leads.

Presenting a polished proposal is key to getting more cleaning clients, especially for larger commercial cleaning contracts where multiple vendors are being considered.

## Bid Smart: Don’t Just Be the Cheapest Option

Pricing is where many cleaners go wrong. Either they underprice to win the job, or overcharge without justifying the value. If you're serious about how to win cleaning contracts, you need to be smart, not cheap.

Here’s how to get it right:

* Know your costs: Labor, cleaning products, insurance, admin, transport.
* Set your profit margin: Typically 15–30% depending on contract size.
* Use a calculator: Avoid emotional pricing: use a simple Google Sheet or free calculator to determine exact costs per job.
* Offer packages: Basic, Standard, Premium tiers allow you to upsell while still appealing to budget-conscious clients.

Explain the value behind your pricing:

“We only use hospital-grade disinfectants and our staff are background checked and uniformed. That’s why we’re priced above market, and why clients stay for years.”

To grow your cleaning business, you have to price like a professional, not a freelancer. Long-term profits come from healthy margins, not bargain rates.

## Keep Clients Longer with Quality & Referrals

Getting clients is hard. Keeping them should be your main focus once they sign. If you're looking at how to grow your cleaning business sustainably, your real profit lies in long-term contracts and referrals.

#### Retain Clients with Quality:

* Use inspection software or checklists to monitor job quality.
* Share monthly or biweekly reports.
* Check in with clients often: “How are things going? Anything you’d like improved?”

Clients who feel cared for rarely leave.

#### Ask for Referrals:

* After 30–60 days of good service, ask:

“If you know any other businesses that could use our help, I’d be happy to offer them a free walkthrough and you a referral discount.”

You’d be surprised how many will help if you simply ask.

Referrals are one of the fastest ways to get more cleaning clients because they come with built-in trust. Pair this with a polished online presence and you’ll naturally start winning more commercial cleaning contracts without spending more on ads.
