## The 2025 Reality: Security Manager Challenges You Can’t Ignore

Security managers today are expected to deliver more with less — fewer supervisors, tighter budgets, and clients who expect real-time updates. Whether you’re running a small local team or managing hundreds of guards across multiple sites, these are the seven security manager challenges you’ll face most often.

### 1) High Turnover & Absenteeism

One of the biggest challenges in managing security guards is simply keeping them. [Turnover rates in security can exceed 100% annually](https://www.belfrysoftware.com/blog/security-guard-turnover#:~:text=Security%20industry%20turnover%20rates:%20A,substantial%20recruitment%20and%20training%20costs.). That means many companies replace their entire guard force every year.

Why this matters:

* Constant recruiting, onboarding, and training cost time and money.
* Frequent staff changes hurt consistency and client trust.

**Quick fix:** Track early warning signs like repeated lates or last-minute shift cancellations, and pair new hires with an experienced “buddy” guard for their first month.

### 2) Security Guard Scheduling Problems

From shift swaps to emergency cover, credential expiries to overtime limits — security guard scheduling problems are a daily headache.

Why this matters:

* Double-bookings and compliance violations can lead to fines or SLA breaches.
* Overtime spikes eat into already tight margins.

**Quick fix:** Use a scheduling system that automatically blocks unlicensed guards from being assigned and alerts managers to potential overtime before it happens.

### 3) SOP Drift & Communication Overload

A key security workforce management challenge is ensuring guards follow the right procedures every time. SOPs can easily get lost in emails or group chats, leading to inconsistent operations.

Why this matters:

* Site-specific rules may be ignored.
* Incident response can vary depending on who’s on duty.

**Quick fix:** Keep SOPs in a centralized platform that’s instantly updated and requires guards to acknowledge receipt.

### 4) Rising Client Expectations

Clients now expect live access to patrol and incident reports, rapid escalation for critical events, and data-backed performance insights. Meeting these expectations is one of the fastest-growing security manager challenges.

**Quick fix:** Offer client portals so they can view shift logs, patrol coverage, and incident status anytime — without waiting for email updates.

### 5) Proof-of-Service Pressure

Without GPS-tracked patrols, timestamped logs, or incident media, it’s hard to prove guards were where they should be. This proof-of-work expectation is becoming a baseline in security workforce management.

**Quick fix:** Adopt guard tour systems with GPS/NFC checkpoints, mobile daily activity reports, and photo/video uploads.

### 6) Managing Multiple Sites with Limited Staff

In multi-site setups, challenges in managing security guards multiply — especially when one manager oversees several locations.

**Quick fix:** Use a unified operations dashboard to track patrol completion, missed checkpoints, and open incidents across all sites in real time.

### 7) Compliance & Safety Risks

From labor laws to licensing, missing a compliance requirement can mean fines or lost contracts, making it one of the most costly security manager challenges.

**Quick fix:** Automate credential tracking with expiry reminders and block scheduling for non-compliant guards.
