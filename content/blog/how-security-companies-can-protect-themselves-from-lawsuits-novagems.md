## Common Legal Risks Security Companies Face

Security companies work in one of the most legally vexed businesses. Any action taken by a guard, any contact with a client, and every report can be perilous. Here are the most prevalent legal traps that create lawsuits and how to avoid them.

* **Negligence and Use of Force Claims**

Negligence cases usually result from allegations that guards have not responded in the proper way or have applied excessive force. Such cases can be ruinous to a security company's bottom line and reputation. Even if your personnel responded appropriately, without a thorough, dated incident report, it may be tough to demonstrate that in court.

***Best Practice:*** Document each incident immediately via a mobile reporting system with photos, videos, and witness statements. Electronic records supported by GPS evidence are irrefutable evidence of your crew's professionalism.

* **Inadequate Training or Licensing Issues**

Working with unlicensed or untrained guards is one of the quickest routes to getting sued—or losing your contract. Canada's, the U.S.'s, and the U.K.'s compliance laws all mandate guards to have current licenses and certifications.

If a single license expires without notice, your insurance may become null. Avoid this by using automated tracking of licenses that notify you when certifications are about to expire. [Novagems ](https://novagems.com/)provides automatic reminders for renewal. Hence, ensuring your team remains compliant without the need for manual tracking.

* **False Arrest and Privacy Violations**

Security officers aren’t police officers, but clients and the public sometimes forget that. If a guard detains or questions someone improperly, it can lead to false arrest or privacy violation claims.

The primary defense in this case is well-documented post orders and guard training. Ensure your personnel understand their legal limits and can present evidence that they took official action. Novagems' Post Orders function ensures guards have recent instructions available to them on their mobile app.

* **Inadequate Documentation or Missing Incident Reports**

When liability protection for security companies is concerned, documentation is important. Incomplete or missing incident reports might put your company in the wrong light, even when guards responded sensibly.

The cloud-based reporting system with auto-seconds and location time stamps saves all reports safely and available for easy review — a significant boon in the event of insurance claims or litigation.
