### Types of Guard Tour Systems

Not all systems are created equal. Here are the most popular types of guard tour systems used today:

#### 1. RFID / iButton Systems

Guards scan a physical tag at each checkpoint. Data is stored on a handheld reader and uploaded later. Reliable, but outdated and lacks real-time features. These use handheld scanners and physical tags placed at checkpoints. Guards scan the tags during patrols. While accurate, these systems usually require uploading data manually from the device.

#### 2. QR Code Systems

Simple to use. QR codes are placed at patrol points. Guards scan them with a mobile app connected to the cloud. Great for smaller teams or cost-conscious operations. QR codes are placed at each location. Guards scan them using a smartphone app. This is cost-effective and works well with cloud-based reporting.

#### 3. GPS-Based Guard Tour Apps

These allow live tracking of guards using GPS on their smartphones. Supervisors can see real-time patrol progress, ideal for large outdoor areas or mobile patrols. These apps track the guard’s real-time location using GPS. Checkpoints can be geofenced, and supervisors can view movements on a live map. Ideal for large outdoor sites or mobile patrols.

#### 4. Hybrid Systems

Many platforms now combine QR, GPS, RFID, and NFC for flexibility across indoor and outdoor locations. Some platforms combine GPS, QR, and RFID features for flexibility. These are great for businesses with varied security needs or large teams.
