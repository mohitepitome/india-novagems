## The $0 Cleaning Startup Plan: Step-by-Step

If you want to start a cleaning business for free, the key is to launch before spending. This plan keeps cleaning business startup costs at zero until you’ve earned your first payments.

### Step 1: Pre-Sell Before You Buy

* Offer friends, family, or neighbors a discounted “first clean” and collect payment upfront.
* Use that payment to fund any minimal supplies or transportation costs.
* This approach removes financial risk and proves there’s demand.

### Step 2: Set Up Free Lead Channels

You can get your first customers without paid ads by leveraging free cleaning business marketing platforms:

* Google Business Profile (GBP) – Appear on Google Maps and local search. Add photos, your services, and hours.
* Nextdoor Business Page – Connect with neighbors and offer an introductory deal.
* Facebook Groups – Join local community groups and post your offer.
* Craigslist Services Section – Free local ad placement.

### Step 3: Use Client-Supplied Supplies

One of the easiest ways to keep cleaning business startup costs at zero is to clean with the customer’s products. Many homeowners prefer this anyway, and it saves you hundreds of dollars upfront.

### Step 4: Create a Simple Pricing Formula

Pricing is critical when running a cleaning business on a budget:

1. Decide your hourly rate (check local averages, often $25–$70/hour).
2. Estimate hours per job.
3. Add 10–20% for overhead (transport, laundry).
4. Offer recurring discounts to lock in regular customers.

### Step 5: Collect Reviews and Referrals Fast

Ask for a review after every job. Offer a small bonus (discount or free add-on) for referrals. This free method builds your credibility without spending on marketing.

## Pricing Your First 10 Cleaning Jobs

Here’s a simple method to keep things profitable when running a cleaning business on a budget:

1. Pick an hourly target rate – Example: $30/hour.
2. Estimate time – 3 hours = $90.
3. Add overhead – +15% = $103.50.
4. Set flat rates – E.g., $100 for a 2BR/2BA, $150 for deep clean.
5. Offer recurring discounts – Keeps clients coming back.

This formula works whether you’re running a side hustle or scaling up.
