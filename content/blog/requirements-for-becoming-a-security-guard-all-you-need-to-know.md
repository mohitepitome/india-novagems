## Requirements To Become A Security Guard

If you are someone who is [residing in Canada](https://novagems.com/canadian-laws-security-guards-need-to-know-about/) or USA then these requirements are going to be useful for you.

### Step 1 - Meet The Basic Requirements

**Age-**

To become a security guard, you must meet minimum age requirements, which typically range from 18 to 21 years, depending on the state or country. Additionally, a clean criminal record is often a prerequisite, particularly when applying for positions that involve carrying firearms or providing high-security services.

Example: In California, a person must be at least 18 years old and must pass a background check before they can receive a security guard license ([BSIS, California](https://www.bsis.ca.gov/forms_pubs/guard_fact.shtml?utm_source=chatgpt.com)).

**Criminal Record-**

You must have a clean criminal record and no cases shall be registered against you. These records might be checked by local state police or the local security guard licensing agency. 

**Citizenship**

You must be a legal citizen of the country. Or you must be legally allowed to work in the country. 

**Language**

You must be able to speak and write American English. 

**State Government Approved Security License Course**

To obtain a security license in the United States or Canada, you must successfully finish an authorized security license course recognized by the respective state. You will need to provide evidence of completing the course, which can include online security license courses. It is advisable to reach out to the relevant state government organization responsible for security licensing to obtain a catalog of approved courses.

### Step 2 - Complete The Training 

In Canada, security guards are required to at least complete the basic training to acquire a security license. The number of hours required may vary depending on different states. But each and every guard must complete “Basic Security Training (BST)”

The security guard needs to complete a 40-hour security license training program with basic CPR and first aid certification.

While a high school diploma or equivalent is typically required, most states also require security guards to complete certified security guard training programs. This training covers essential topics like conflict resolution, report writing, physical security, and emergency procedures.

Training hours vary by state; for example, California requires a minimum of 40 hours of training, which must be completed over six months. This includes classroom instruction and hands-on training ([BSIS, California](https://www.bsis.ca.gov/forms_pubs/guard_fact.shtml?utm_source=chatgpt.com)).

### Step 3 - Obtain A Security Guard License  

After completing the required training, aspiring security guards must apply for a state-issued security guard license. This license is a legal requirement to work as a security guard in most states and may involve additional checks, such as fingerprinting or medical evaluations for fitness.

Example: In New York, applicants must submit an application to the New York State Division of Licensing Services, pass a background check, and complete required training hours ([NYS Division of Licensing Services](https://www.dos.ny.gov/licensing/security_guard/security_guard.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)).

## Key Skills and Qualities of a Successful Security Guard

Security guards need to possess certain personal qualities and soft skills to succeed in their role. These traits help ensure that security personnel not only meet physical demands but also handle a variety of challenges they may encounter on the job.

### 1. Communication Skills

A security guard must be able to communicate clearly and effectively, both with clients and the general public. This includes being able to write detailed incident reports and de-escalate potentially volatile situations through conversation.

Example: A security guard at a mall might be asked to diffuse an argument between two individuals. Having strong communication skills can prevent escalation and keep the situation calm.

### 2. Problem-Solving Abilities

Security guards are often faced with unexpected situations, such as accidents, emergencies, or potential security threats. A quick thinker with strong problem-solving skills can make decisions rapidly, ensuring that the situation is handled in the best way possible.

Example: If a security guard notices suspicious behavior in a parking lot, they need to assess the situation quickly and decide whether to confront the individual, call the police, or alert their team.

### 3. Customer Service Skills

While security guards are responsible for maintaining safety, they are often the first point of contact for people entering a facility. Offering good customer service helps create a positive environment while also fulfilling their security responsibilities.

Example: Greeting visitors politely and providing clear directions enhances the client experience, especially in large office buildings or event venues.

### 4. Attention to Detail

An observant security guard can spot potential threats or violations of protocol by noticing small details that others may miss. This skill helps in preventing incidents and ensuring that all areas are secure.

### Career Advancement in the Security Industry

Security guard jobs are not just limited to entry-level positions. With experience and the right certifications, security guards can climb the career ladder. Here are a few career advancement options:

#### 1. Specialization in Executive Protection

After gaining experience, many security guards transition to more specialized roles, such as executive protection. These positions involve safeguarding high-profile individuals and require specialized training in close protection and risk management.

#### 2. Cybersecurity Roles

With the growing reliance on digital systems, the cybersecurity industry is booming. Experienced security professionals with knowledge of IT and network security can transition into cybersecurity roles, such as security analysts or network administrators.

#### 3. Becoming a Security Supervisor or Manager

Security guards with extensive experience may be promoted to managerial roles, such as security supervisor or security manager, where they oversee the work of other security personnel and manage operations for large facilities or events.

### Salary Expectations and Job Outlook

#### 1. Salary Expectations

The salary of a security guard can vary based on location, experience, and job type. On average, a security guard in the United States earns around $38,370 per year, with higher pay for specialized roles like armed security or executive protection.

Example: In metropolitan areas like New York or Los Angeles, security guards may earn higher salaries due to increased demand and cost of living adjustments.

#### 2. Job Outlook

The job outlook for security guards is positive, with a projected growth rate of 2.2% annually between 2020 and 2030. This reflects increasing demand for security services in both private and public sectors, particularly with heightened concerns over safety in public spaces.

Example: The increasing number of businesses, government buildings, and public events is expected to keep the demand for security guards steady over the next decade ([Bureau of Labor Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/protective-service/security-guards.htm)).
