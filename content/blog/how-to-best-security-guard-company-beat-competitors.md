**7. Offer Competitive Pricing**

You can always win the competition by offering affordable rates. However, before you start selling your services for cheap, you must know the ideal price point. Understanding the prices of the competition’s goods and services allows you to derive the best value for your product. You must always try and bring more value to the table even if you have to price your services higher for this.

Using a [competition-based pricing strategy](https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/pricing-strategy) often helps in gaining an advantage. However, to follow this strategy, you must have a clear understanding of your audience and the group they belong to i.e. lower, middle, and upper-tiered customers based on pricing.

Competing on price alone is a losing game. Instead:

* Understand competitor pricing but design your price for margin + value.
* Use value-based pricing — charge more for enhancements clients truly value (faster response, transparency, integrated tech).
* Use tiered pricing — allow clients to select from Silver / Gold / Platinum packages.
* Consider performance-based pricing or bonuses tied to KPIs (incident reduction, uptime, guard attendance).

Avoid deep discounting — it can cheapen your brand. Instead offer discounts for longer contracts or added scope.

### Frequently Asked Questions 

#### How do I truly differentiate a security guard company?

By combining specialization, technology, transparent reporting, reputation, and exceptional client experience — not just price.

#### What is a realistic tech investment ROI?

While it depends on scale, many firms see cost savings (less overtime, fewer errors) and higher contract retention within 6–12 months of tech adoption.

#### Can small security firms compete with large ones?

Yes — by being nimble, focusing on a niche, customizing service, and delivering better client experience rather than competing head-to-head on volume.

#### How often should I review competitive analysis?

At least quarterly. Monitor competitor offerings, pricing, client complaints, and new entrants to stay ahead.

#### Should I reduce price to win deals?

Only if you can still maintain margin and deliver on quality. Better is packaging value (transparency, tech, guaranteed response) rather than discounting.
